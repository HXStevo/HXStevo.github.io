✈️ Gatwick Departures

Live flights, terminal info, and everything you need before you fly

📍 London Gatwick Airport (LGW) — the UK's second busiest airport, serving over 40 million passengers a year

Gatwick handles flights from two terminals, each with its own character and airline mix. Whether you're heading to the Canaries with easyJet or catching a BA flight to the Med, knowing your terminal makes everything smoother.

Below you'll find a full guide to both terminals, followed by today's live departure board. Less hassle. More holiday.

The Terminals

North Terminal

North Terminal

Opened 1988 — Home to easyJet, Swiss, China Eastern & more

The North Terminal is easyJet's main Gatwick hub. If you're flying short-haul to Europe on a budget, chances are you'll depart from here. It's also home to Swiss, China Eastern (Shanghai direct), and several other carriers.

The terminal underwent a major refurbishment and offers a solid selection of shops and restaurants airside. Security queues tend to move quickly, and the gate areas are well-spaced. If you're driving, the North Terminal has its own multi-storey car park and Meet & Greet options.

Key airlines: easyJet, SWISS, China Eastern, Aurigny

South Terminal

South Terminal

Opened 1958 — Home to British Airways, Ryanair, Vueling, Turkish Airlines & more

The South Terminal is the original Gatwick terminal — the one connected directly to the train station. British Airways operates most of its Gatwick flights from here, alongside Ryanair, Vueling, Turkish Airlines, Norwegian, and Wizz Air.

If you're arriving by train or Gatwick Express, the South Terminal is right there — no transit needed. It's generally busier than the North, but the recent upgrades to security (with new CT scanners) have sped things up considerably.

Key airlines: British Airways, Ryanair, Vueling, Turkish Airlines, Norwegian, Wizz Air

Getting between terminals: A free shuttle train runs between North and South every few minutes. Journey time is about two minutes. If you're at the wrong terminal, don't panic — it's a quick fix. Just allow 15-20 minutes to be safe.

Planning Your Departure

⏰ When to Arrive

Gatwick recommends arriving at least two hours before short-haul flights and three hours before long-haul. During peak times (early morning and late afternoon), security queues can build up — arriving early gives you breathing room and time to grab a coffee airside.

🅿️ Parking Options

Both terminals have short-stay, long-stay, and Meet & Greet parking. Pre-booking saves up to 60% compared to turning up on the day. Long-stay car parks connect to the terminals via free shuttle buses running every few minutes.

→ Search Gatwick parking deals

🚂 By Train

The Gatwick Express from London Victoria takes 30 minutes and drops you right at the South Terminal. Thameslink and Southern services also stop at Gatwick. If you're heading to the North Terminal, it's a quick shuttle ride from the station.

🛍️ Airside Shopping

Both terminals have World Duty Free, a solid selection of high-street brands, and plenty of food options. The North Terminal's refurbished departures lounge is particularly good — if you've got time to kill, you won't be bored.
