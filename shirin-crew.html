Breakfast at Tiffany's

1961 · Director: Blake Edwards · Starring: Audrey Hepburn, George Peppard

One of cinema's most iconic films, Breakfast at Tiffany's follows the enchanting Holly Golightly — a free-spirited New York socialite navigating life with style, wit, and a little chaos. Audrey Hepburn's performance is nothing short of legendary.

The film is famous for the opening scene of Holly eating a pastry outside Tiffany's jewellery store on Fifth Avenue at dawn — a moment of pure cinematic magic. Henry Mancini's hauntingly beautiful score "Moon River" won the Academy Award for Best Original Song.

🏆 Academy Award 🎵 Moon River 👗 Audrey Hepburn 🗽 New York 1961 Classic
🌙

Moon River

The iconic song was written specifically for Audrey Hepburn's voice range. Studio executives wanted it cut — Hepburn's response was reportedly unprintable. It stayed in. Good call.

👗

The Little Black Dress

The Givenchy black dress Hepburn wore in the opening scene sold at auction in 2006 for £467,200 — making it one of the most valuable pieces of film costume ever sold.

💛

Tiffany Blue

The distinctive robin's-egg blue used by Tiffany & Co. became iconic partly through this film. The colour is trademarked — Pantone 1837, named after the year Tiffany was founded.

🐱

The Cat Has No Name

Holly Golightly's cat is simply called "Cat" — because she refuses to give it a name until she's ready to commit. One of the film's most endearing subplots.
