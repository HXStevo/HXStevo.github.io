London Heathrow is the busiest airport in the United Kingdom, serving over 80 million passengers every year across four active terminals. Whether you need a comfortable room the night before an early departure or a convenient hotel and parking package that keeps your car secure while you travel, there are more than 120 hotels near Heathrow Airport to suit every budget and terminal. From on-airport hotels connected by walkway to affordable options along Bath Road served by the free Hoppa shuttle bus, this guide covers everything you need to find and book the right Heathrow airport hotel.

We specialise in airport hotels across the UK, and Heathrow is our most popular destination. Below you will find hotels organised by terminal, side-by-side comparisons, details on hotel and parking packages, and answers to the 20 most common questions travellers ask about staying near Heathrow.

Hotels by terminal

Heathrow airport hotels near every terminal

Heathrow has four active terminals. Choosing a hotel close to your departure terminal saves time and stress on the morning of your flight. Here is what each terminal serves and the best hotels nearby.

Terminal 2

Star Alliance · United · Lufthansa · Air Canada

The Queen's Terminal handles Star Alliance carriers. The Hilton Garden Inn London Heathrow sits directly beside the terminal with a covered walkway connection, making it the closest hotel to Terminal 2. The Holiday Inn Express T2 and ibis Styles are affordable alternatives with Hoppa access.

Terminal 3

Virgin Atlantic · Emirates · American Airlines · Cathay Pacific

Terminal 3 is the long-haul hub for non-alliance carriers. The Hilton London Heathrow is opposite Terminal 3 and connected by covered walkway. Along Bath Road, the Leonardo Hotel and Crowne Plaza both offer rooms from under £80 with frequent Hoppa shuttle service.

Terminal 4

Qatar Airways · Malaysia Airlines · KLM

Terminal 4 sits south of the main Heathrow campus. The Hilton London Heathrow Terminal 4 and Premier Inn T4 are both within walking distance. For hotel and parking packages at Terminal 4, the Holiday Inn Heathrow M4 and Thistle London Heathrow T5 offer excellent value with park-and-fly deals.

Terminal 5

British Airways — exclusive home terminal

Terminal 5 is the flagship British Airways terminal. The Sofitel London Heathrow connects directly via walkway and is the only hotel inside the airport perimeter. The Holiday Inn Express T5 offers budget-friendly rooms with Hoppa access, while Yotel pods inside T5 are perfect for layovers.

Hotels inside Heathrow Airport

Heathrow has a small number of hotels that are physically inside or directly connected to the terminal buildings. The Sofitel London Heathrow at Terminal 5 is connected by a covered walkway, allowing you to walk from your room to check-in in under five minutes without stepping outside. The Hilton Garden Inn at Terminal 2 and the Hilton Terminal 4 also border their respective terminals with short, sheltered walks. For travellers between flights, Yotel operates compact sleep cabins inside both Terminal 4 and Terminal 5 that can be booked by the hour.

On-airport hotels are the premium choice for travellers who want to minimise transit time and avoid shuttle buses entirely. They come at a higher nightly rate — typically starting from £120 to £160 — but the convenience of rolling your luggage straight to the departure lounge is hard to beat, especially for early morning British Airways departures from Terminal 5.

Hotel & parking packages

Heathrow airport hotel and parking packages

A Heathrow airport hotel and parking package bundles one night of accommodation with secured parking for 8 to 15 days. You drive to the hotel the evening before your flight, enjoy a comfortable night's rest, leave your car in the hotel's monitored car park, and take the Hoppa shuttle or walk to your terminal. When you return from your trip, the shuttle brings you back to collect your car. It is the most convenient and cost-effective way to start a holiday from Heathrow, saving up to 60% compared with booking the hotel and long-stay airport parking separately.

Park and ride hotel packages

Park and ride packages pair your hotel stay with off-airport parking at a dedicated compound, usually within five to ten minutes of the terminal. A regular shuttle bus runs between the car park and all four terminals throughout the day and night. This is the most affordable option for budget-conscious travellers who want the convenience of a pre-flight hotel stay without paying premium on-airport parking rates. Prices start from around £55 for the hotel and 8 days of parking combined, making it significantly cheaper than booking each element separately.

Meet and greet hotel packages

If you prefer a hands-free experience, meet and greet packages add a valet service to your hotel stay. You drive directly to the terminal on the morning of your flight, hand your keys to a uniformed driver, and walk straight into departures. Your car is taken to a secured compound and returned to the terminal forecourt when you land. Meet and greet is the premium option — ideal for families with young children or business travellers who want to avoid shuttle buses entirely. Typical meet and greet hotel packages at Heathrow start from around £95 for one night plus 8 days of parking.

The Sofitel London Heathrow is a five-star property connected to Terminal 5 with a spa, fine dining and sound-proofed rooms. The Hilton London Heathrow opposite Terminal 3 offers executive lounges and upgraded rooms. The Crowne Plaza and Marriott on Bath Road both provide four-star service with conference facilities, making them popular with business travellers. Premium rooms at Heathrow typically range from £130 to £250 per night depending on season and demand.

Getting to the terminal

The Hoppa shuttle bus at Heathrow

The Hoppa bus is the main transfer service connecting off-airport hotels to all four Heathrow terminals. Buses run every 15 to 20 minutes from early morning until late at night, and the journey from Bath Road hotels to the nearest terminal takes around 5 to 10 minutes. Many hotel and parking packages include the Hoppa fare in the price, so there is nothing extra to pay. Simply walk to the bus stop outside your hotel and board the next available service — no pre-booking is required.

If your hotel has its own complimentary minibus, that will often be even quicker. Properties like the Radisson Blu Edwardian and Marriott Heathrow run their own transfer shuttles to Terminal 3 on demand. For on-airport hotels connected by walkway — the Sofitel at T5, Hilton at T3 and Hilton Garden Inn at T2 — no shuttle is needed at all.

Bath Road — Heathrow's hotel strip

Bath Road (A4) runs parallel to the northern runway and is home to the largest concentration of hotels near Heathrow Airport. From the Sheraton and Marriott at the western end to the Leonardo and Holiday Inn further east, Bath Road offers more than 20 properties within a two-mile stretch. All are connected to the terminals by the Hoppa shuttle bus. Bath Road hotels tend to be more affordable than on-airport options because they are a short drive from the terminal rather than a walk — making them the sweet spot between price and convenience for most travellers.

Early check-in and late checkout at Heathrow hotels

Many Heathrow airport hotels cater specifically to travellers with unsociable flight times. Early check-in from midday is standard at most properties, but several — including Premier Inn and Travelodge — offer check-in from as early as 2pm with no extra charge. For very early departures, some hotels near Heathrow airport provide 24-hour check-in or dedicated early-arrival rooms from 6am. Late checkout can usually be arranged at reception on the morning of departure for a small fee, which is useful if your return flight lands in the morning and you want to freshen up before driving home.

Traveller types

Family-friendly hotels near Heathrow Airport

Travelling with children adds complexity to any trip, and choosing the right hotel makes a real difference. The Holiday Inn Heathrow M4 Junction 4 and Novotel London Heathrow both offer interconnecting family rooms, kids-eat-free restaurant policies and dedicated play areas. Premier Inn family rooms comfortably sleep two adults and two children at a fixed nightly rate, making budgeting straightforward. For families driving to the airport, a hotel and parking package eliminates the stress of finding and paying for long-stay parking separately — you simply drive to the hotel, unload the car, and everything is taken care of.

Business hotels near Heathrow

Heathrow is a major gateway for corporate travel, and several nearby hotels cater specifically to business guests. The Crowne Plaza, Marriott and Sheraton on Bath Road all offer conference suites, executive lounges, high-speed Wi-Fi and work desks in every room. The Hilton London Heathrow adjacent to Terminal 3 is a favourite for business travellers flying long-haul with Virgin Atlantic or Emirates, combining proximity to the terminal with full-service facilities. Many business hotels also offer flexible cancellation policies and corporate rates when booked through a travel management platform.

Heathrow hotels with breakfast included

Starting your trip with a proper breakfast sets you up for the journey ahead. Budget chains like Premier Inn and Holiday Inn Express include a full or continental breakfast in the room rate as standard — no extra charge. At mid-range and premium hotels, breakfast can typically be added during booking for around £10 to £15 per person. Several hotel and parking packages bundle breakfast into the overall price, giving you accommodation, parking and a morning meal all in one transaction. If an early flight means you need to leave before the restaurant opens, many Heathrow hotels offer a grab-and-go breakfast bag that you can collect from reception the night before.

Pet-friendly hotels near Heathrow

If you are dropping off or collecting a pet at Heathrow's animal reception centre, a handful of nearby hotels welcome dogs and other pets. The Novotel London Heathrow and certain Premier Inn locations accept dogs for a small nightly surcharge, typically around £10 to £15. It is always best to confirm pet policies directly with the hotel when booking, as rules on breed size and the number of animals allowed can vary. Hotels near Heathrow airport with parking facilities are particularly convenient for pet owners, as you can keep your car close and transport your animal comfortably.

Hotels for early morning flights from Heathrow

If your departure is before 9am, staying the night before at a Heathrow hotel is the most reliable way to avoid the unpredictability of early morning motorway traffic and M25 delays. Hotels connected directly to a terminal — the Sofitel at T5, Hilton at T3 and Hilton Garden Inn at T2 — let you walk to check-in within minutes of leaving your room. Off-airport hotels with Hoppa shuttle access start running buses from around 4am, ensuring you can reach even the earliest departures with time to spare. Booking a hotel and parking package the night before a dawn flight means you arrive rested, your car is secure, and there is no rush.
