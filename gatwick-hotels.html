Finding the Right Gatwick Hotel

Flying from Gatwick? A pre-flight hotel stay removes the stress entirely. No 3am alarm. No M25 anxiety. Just a good night's sleep and a short walk (or shuttle) to check-in. Whether you're after five-star luxury or a budget pod, there's a Gatwick hotel for every traveller and every budget.

The big decision is on-airport vs off-airport. On-airport hotels sit within or directly connected to the terminals. You're talking a 2-minute walk to departures. The trade-off? Higher prices — typically £80-£150+ per night. But for early morning flights or late arrivals, that convenience is hard to beat.

Off-airport hotels are located in nearby Crawley or along the Manor Royal business district, usually 5-15 minutes by free shuttle bus. Rates start from just £40/night, and many include parking for the duration of your trip. If you're budget-conscious or don't mind a short transfer, these deliver serious value.

Here's what to consider: Flight time (early = on-airport), budget (off-airport saves 30-50%), terminal (South has more options), and extras like pools, parking, or family rooms. We've reviewed all 10 options below so you can make the right call.

On-Airport Hotels

Walk to the terminal. No shuttle. No stress. These hotels are directly connected to (or inside) Gatwick's terminals.

Sofitel London Gatwick

★★★★★
South Terminal On-Airport

The only 5-star hotel at Gatwick, and it shows. The Sofitel is connected directly to South Terminal via a covered walkway — you're at check-in within 2 minutes. With 518 rooms, a full-service spa, and three restaurants, this is premium pre-flight living.

Rooms are soundproofed (essential at an airport), with luxury bedding, rainfall showers, and Hermès toiletries. The La Terrasse restaurant serves excellent French-Mediterranean cuisine, while the cocktail bar is perfect for a pre-holiday drink. Business travellers love the executive lounge.

Best for: Business travellers, couples, anyone wanting a luxury start to their trip. The spa alone is worth arriving early for.

£150 / night (avg)
4.3 / 5 · 518 rooms

Hilton London Gatwick Airport

★★★★
South Terminal On-Airport

Gatwick's largest hotel with 821 rooms, the Hilton is connected to South Terminal by a covered walkway. It's the go-to for families thanks to its indoor swimming pool, kids' menu, and spacious family rooms. The Amy's Bar & Kitchen serves comfort food that actually delivers.

Rooms are modern Hilton standard — clean, reliable, well-equipped. Executive rooms add lounge access with complimentary drinks and snacks. The gym and sauna round out the leisure offering. Underground car park available for hotel-and-parking packages.

Best for: Families, group travellers, anyone wanting the pool. The hotel-and-parking packages are some of the best value at Gatwick.

£120 / night (avg)
4.0 / 5 · 821 rooms

BLOC Hotel Gatwick

★★★
South Terminal On-Airport Airside

This is unique. BLOC sits inside South Terminal, past security. Check in to your room, sleep, wake up, and you're already airside — walk straight to your gate. No queues. No stress. It's the ultimate convenience play.

Rooms are compact but smart. Think pod-hotel-meets-boutique: mood lighting controlled by a bedside tablet, powerful rain showers, blackout blinds, and surprisingly comfortable beds. No restaurant, but you've got the terminal's food options right outside.

Best for: Solo travellers, early flights, transit passengers. You need a valid boarding pass to access the hotel — remember, it's airside.

£80 / night (avg)
4.1 / 5 · 245 rooms

Hampton by Hilton London Gatwick Airport

★★★
North Terminal On-Airport

North Terminal's only on-airport hotel. Connected via a covered walkway, the Hampton delivers Hilton quality at a mid-range price. The big draw? Free hot breakfast included with every booking. That alone saves £10-15 per person.

Rooms are modern, clean, and functional. Not flashy, but everything works. King and twin rooms available, plus accessible options. Free WiFi, a small gym, and a 24-hour snack area. The location is unbeatable for North Terminal flyers.

Best for: North Terminal passengers, budget-conscious travellers who still want on-airport convenience, families who appreciate free breakfast.

£95 / night (avg)
4.2 / 5 · 168 rooms

Yotel Gatwick

★★
South Terminal On-Airport Airside

Not a hotel in the traditional sense — Yotel offers compact pods (or "cabins") inside South Terminal. Available both landside and airside, bookable by the hour or overnight. It's the cheapest on-airport sleep at Gatwick.

Cabins are tiny but clever. A fold-down bed, ensuite shower, TV, WiFi, and adjustable mood lighting. The monster power shower is genuinely impressive for the space. Self check-in kiosks mean zero waiting. Landside cabins don't need a boarding pass.

Best for: Budget travellers, layover naps, early flights where you just need a few hours. Hourly rates start from around £30 for a 4-hour block.

£50 / night (avg)
3.8 / 5 · Pods

Off-Airport Hotels

Lower prices, free shuttle buses, and often free parking included. A 5-15 minute transfer to the terminal — great value for money.

Courtyard by Marriott London Gatwick

★★★★
Off-Airport

The best off-airport option if you want Marriott reliability without the premium price. Located on the Manor Royal business estate, it's a 10-minute shuttle ride to both terminals. Modern rooms with Marriott's signature bedding — consistently comfortable.

The Bistro restaurant serves decent international food, and the bar is well-stocked. Free parking available when booked as a hotel-and-parking package. The lobby has a relaxed coworking vibe that suits business travellers. Shuttle runs every 20 minutes.

Best for: Business travellers, Marriott loyalists, anyone wanting 4-star quality at off-airport prices.

£90 / night (avg)
4.2 / 5

Premier Inn London Gatwick Airport (Manor Royal)

★★★
Off-Airport

You know what you're getting with Premier Inn. Clean rooms, the famous Hypnos beds, and a hot breakfast next door at the Beefeater restaurant. It's reliable, consistent, and won't break the bank. The shuttle to both terminals runs every 20 minutes.

Family rooms sleep two adults and two kids. Parking packages available. The rooms are a decent size, WiFi is free, and the blackout curtains actually work. It's not glamorous, but it does the job brilliantly.

Best for: Families on a budget, anyone who values reliability over frills, Premier Inn regulars.

£55 / night (avg)
4.0 / 5

Travelodge London Gatwick Airport Central

★★
Off-Airport

The cheapest option near Gatwick. From £40 a night, you get a clean room, a comfortable bed, and a shuttle to the airport. That's it. And honestly? For a pre-flight sleep, that's all you need.

Rooms are basic but functional. King-size beds in every room, power shower, TV, and free WiFi. Breakfast isn't included but can be added for around £9. The café next door is a cheaper alternative. Shuttle service to both terminals.

Best for: Budget travellers who just need a bed, solo travellers, anyone who'd rather spend money at the destination.

£40 / night (avg)
3.5 / 5

Arora Hotel Gatwick/Crawley

★★★★
Off-Airport

A hidden gem. The Arora offers genuine 4-star facilities at off-airport prices — including an indoor pool, spa, and well-equipped gym. That's a combination you won't find at this price point anywhere else near Gatwick.

Rooms are spacious and well-appointed. The restaurant serves solid British and international cuisine, and the bar has a decent cocktail menu. Free shuttle to both terminals every 15-20 minutes. Parking packages available for the duration of your trip.

Best for: Families wanting a pool, couples wanting spa time before a flight, anyone wanting 4-star at 3-star prices.

£85 / night (avg)
4.1 / 5

Gatwick Worth Hotel

★★★
Off-Airport

Character over convenience. The Worth Hotel is a charming period building set in its own grounds — a world away from the usual airport hotel feel. If you want something with personality, this is it.

Rooms blend period features with modern comforts. The restaurant uses local produce, and the gardens are a genuine draw in summer. Free shuttle to both terminals. Parking available. It's slightly further out than other off-airport options, but the atmosphere makes up for it.

Best for: Couples, anyone wanting character, travellers who'd rather not feel like they're in an airport hotel.

£70 / night (avg)
