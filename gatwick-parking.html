Types of Gatwick Airport Parking

Gatwick offers three main types of parking to suit every budget and travel style. Understanding the difference helps you pick the right option for your trip.

Meet & Greet Parking at Gatwick

With Gatwick meet and greet parking, you drive straight to the terminal, hand your keys to a professional driver, and walk directly into departures. On return, your car is delivered back to the arrivals hall. It's the most convenient option — ideal for families with young children, heavy luggage, or those on tight schedules. No shuttle bus, no walking. Just door-to-door service.

Park & Ride Parking at Gatwick

Park and ride is the budget-friendly choice. You drive to an off-airport car park, leave your car in a secure facility, and catch a regular shuttle bus to the terminal. Transfer times range from 5 to 20 minutes depending on the car park. For most travellers, Gatwick park and ride offers the best balance of price and convenience, with frequent shuttles and high customer ratings.

On-Airport Parking at Gatwick

Gatwick's own Short Stay and Long Stay car parks are connected directly to the terminals, making them extremely convenient. However, they're significantly more expensive than pre-booked off-airport options. If you're catching an early morning flight or returning late at night, the convenience can be worth the premium — but always compare prices before you book.

Gatwick South Terminal vs North Terminal Parking

Gatwick Airport has two terminals — South Terminal and North Terminal — connected by a free shuttle train running every few minutes, 24 hours a day. The journey between terminals takes approximately 2 minutes. Most popular airlines, including easyJet and British Airways, use South Terminal. Always check your airline's terminal before booking parking, as it affects which car parks are most convenient for you.

Which airlines use Gatwick South Terminal?

  • easyJet — South Terminal (primary base)
  • British Airways — South Terminal
  • TUI — South Terminal
  • Vueling — South Terminal
  • Wizz Air — South Terminal

Which airlines use Gatwick North Terminal?

  • Norwegian — North Terminal
  • Jet2 — North Terminal
  • Ryanair — North Terminal
  • Virgin Atlantic — North Terminal
  • Delta — North Terminal

If you're unsure which terminal your flight departs from, check your booking confirmation or your airline's website. Both terminals have excellent parking options available.

What to Expect: Gatwick Parking Step by Step

Park & Ride — Step by Step

  1. Drive to the car park using the postcode and directions in your booking confirmation
  2. Check in at the barrier using your booking reference (most car parks use ANPR to recognise your plate automatically)
  3. Park your car in an allocated space
  4. Take your luggage and board the next shuttle bus to the terminal
  5. On return, catch the shuttle back to the car park, collect your keys from reception, and drive home

Meet & Greet — Step by Step

  1. Drive directly to the terminal drop-off zone (follow the signs for your terminal)
  2. Meet your pre-arranged driver — they'll be waiting for you at the agreed location
  3. Hand over your keys, collect your parking receipt, and walk straight into departures
  4. On return, call the number on your receipt from the arrivals hall
  5. Your driver will bring your car directly to the arrivals forecourt — usually within 15–20 minutes

Top Tips for Parking at Gatwick

  • Book 3–4 weeks in advance to secure the best price — the earlier, the cheaper
  • Check your terminal before booking — South and North Terminal have different parking options
  • Allow extra time if using park and ride — add 45 minutes to your terminal arrival calculation
  • Keep your booking confirmation accessible — email or screenshot, not buried in your inbox
  • Check the car park address and postcode — not all sat navs know every car park name
  • Charge your phone before you leave — you'll need it for meet and greet pick-up calls
  • Take a photo of your car and parking space number before leaving — useful on return
  • Read the customer reviews — they tell you what Google Maps won't
